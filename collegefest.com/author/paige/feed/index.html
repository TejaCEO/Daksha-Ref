]]> Are you looking for activities to do in Boston this summer? Get out of your routine and explore the city. Here are a few ideas below to help get you started on your planning.

 

Rent Canoes

Visit Paddle Boston to rent a canoe, kayak or paddle board to spend the afternoon exploring the Boston scenery and beautiful weather this summer. Avoid the busy lines and crowds of tourists as you paddle down the Charles River. You will paddle past many colleges in Boston, the Esplanade, Prudential Building, and so on. Explore the city from the water while experiencing the city rush. Step back and relax on your own time frame on the river. This is a great group activity to do with friends, family and even your dog! If you are interested in purchasing a season pass, the student rate for all the rentals is only $119.00 compared to the regular rate of $219.00. There are a lot of opportunities for students to have fun in Boston and you save some money when doing so.

Credit: Paddle Boston

Walk the Freedom Trail

The Freedom Trail is one of the largest attractions of all the historic landmarks in Boston. Being local, we tend to forget that people come from far and wide to walk this trail and take photographs of all the famous sites along the trail. You can walk along the trail with a map or join a tour group from Boston Common all the way to the Bunker Hill Monument. This trail will bring you back to your Elementary history lesson and to be thankful for this historic landmark being so accessible to us all.

Credit: Boston by Foot

Visit the New England Aquarium

Experience the world under water of Boston Harbor at the New England Aquarium. With over 70 exhibits, there is always the chance to meet the animals and watch them in action. Visiting the aquarium is a great experience whether you have a passion for animals or you want to grow a deeper connection and understanding about a particular species. With a college student I.D there is a discount off one full-priced admission ticket. Check with your student activities department at your school to find out if your school offers additional discounts for their students.

Credit: Duro Terra

Duck Tours 

There is nothing more authentic and classic to do in Boston than a Duck tour. Ride in the renovated World War II vehicle to visit a couple of the historical landmarks and then end your tour in the water for a ride along the Charles River. Throughout the ride there is narration led by the conductor. You’ll learn about all about Boston, ranging from the historical importance of Faneuil Hall to the height of the Prudential Tower and everything in between. Tickets are sold in advance for this great group activity. This is an exciting and memorable experience like no other.

Credit: New Boston Post

]]> Looking to work on that summer bod. Consider these fitness hotspots around Boston.

B/Spoke is a fitness studio on 101 Federal Street in Boston, MA. This high energy fitness studio offers their R/DE class which is a full-body workout on the bike with the newest and best playlists that make 45 minutes of hard work, sweat, and intensity FUN and fly by! B/Spoke offers student discounts changing the single drop-in class price from $29.00 to only $21.00. The student 10 class package is discounted from $250.00 to $190.00. There are other R/DE classes which consist of a heated R/DE class, HIP HOP R/DE, #THROWBACK R/DE, Charity R/DE, 60 minutes, 75 minutes, & 101 minutes for those warriors out there. All of these classes are a great way to start your early morning, spend your lunch break or finish off your long day after work! Be sure to check out this new studio in Boston if you want an invigorating environment where you can test your limits on and off the bike. For more information or to book a class now, visit their site or more information!

Credit:B/Spoke

Pure Barre, located on 350 Newbury Street, Boston, MA 02115, is another great place to take classes and stay fit. During this workout, you can expect to lift light weights at high repetition in order to burn and tone your muscles. Pure Barre is a 45-50 minute class of full body workouts. What is unique to Pure Barre is that it is a low impact workout designed to improve your strength and balance throughout every muscle group. This studio is perfect for every body shape and size. Whether you are coming in before or after your work day, you definitely sweat away the stress from the day and are truly benefiting your body. Pure Barre offers a new client special, one month unlimited for $100.00, so for students in Boston for a summer internship, you have a great deal to take advantage of! Find a studio near you.

Credit: Pure Barre

CrossFitSouthie, located on 385 Dorchester Ave is a high intensity workout involving compound movements through body-weight, weights, and conditioning that is a great workout for anyone. CrossFit has a reputation of being costly, however this location provides students with a 10% discount rate. CrossFit is focused on core strength and conditioning and improves your cardiovascular endurance, strength, power, speed, coordination, agility, balance, accuracy and builds confidence from the inside out. CrossFit stands out compared to a typical fitness facility as they incorporate gymnastics, kettlebells and various forms of weight lifting that will work exclusively with compound movements and shorter high intensity cardio sessions which develops a well-rounded strong athlete. What better way to blow off some steam, and meet new people than getting stronger and lifting at CrossFitSouthie! For more information or to get started click here.

Credit: CrossFitSouthie
