There\u2019s no doubt about it, Summer 2020 is a lot different than the past. But that doesn\u2019t mean that you can\u2019t still have fun! Summer 2020 is definitely not cancelled! So, if you\u2019re still itching to get outside and get out of the house check out these ideas of things to do in\/around Boston this summer.<\/span><\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Go to a Drive-in Movie<\/b>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

This may be an old fashioned way to see a movie, but retro is in! While many regular movie theaters are closed due to COVID-19 restrictions, drive-in movies are fully open for the season. A drive-in is a perfect way to get your movie fix, while still maintaining social distancing.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

New England has plenty of theaters to choose from:<\/span><\/p>\n

Massachusetts:\u00a0<\/b>Leicester Triple Drive-In Theatre<\/span><\/a>, <\/span>Wellfleet Cinemas<\/span><\/a>,\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

New Hampshire:\u00a0<\/b>Northfield Drive-In Theatre<\/span><\/a>, <\/span>Milford Drive-In Theater<\/span><\/a><\/p>\n

Rhode Island (near MA border):<\/b>\u00a0Rustic Tri-View Drive-in<\/span><\/a><\/p>\n

**Make sure to check with your local movie theater. Some theaters are putting on make-shift drive-ins in their parking lots.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Visit an Outdoor Museum\/Garden<\/b>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

Just because some museums are closed, doesn\u2019t mean they all are. There are still so many things to learn and places to explore! Check out one of the many open air museums in\/around the city.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

If you\u2019re interested in history then\u00a0<\/span>Sturbridge Village<\/span><\/a>\u00a0is perfect for you! There you can go back in time and experience what it was like in the 19th centuries in Massachusetts. (Make sure to get your tickets in advance).<\/span><\/p>\n

For people more interested in taking in some beautiful scenery and art, <\/span>Heritage Gardens<\/span><\/a> and the <\/span>deCordova sculpture park<\/span><\/a> are open for business.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

Photo by BostonPhotoSphere on flickr<\/p><\/div>\n

Head to the Water<\/b><\/p>\n

Whether it\u2019s the beach, lake, or river, Boston has it all! Taking a trip to the water can be incredibly restorative, energizing, relaxing, and most importantly, fun!<\/span><\/p>\n

Some great beaches for swimming, relaxing, and amazing views directly in the city are Carson Beach in Southie and Orient Street Beach in East Boston (which has terrific views of the planes taking off and landing at the airport).\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

But if you\u2019re looking for a bit of a beach escape you can easily take a short trip from the city to Marblehead, MA for the deeply gorgeous and refreshing, rocky beaches.\u00a0 Or head to Falmouth, Cape Cod to take in the warmer and softer coastline.<\/span><\/p>\n

For something a bit more active, head down the Charles River where you can rent canoes, paddle boards, and kayaks to take on the river. Just make sure to watch out for the rowers while you\u2019re out there!\u00a0<\/span>Paddle Boston<\/span><\/a> has everything you need for a fun afternoon\/day on the water. They are currently requiring reservations, so make sure to book online or give them a call before you go.<\/span><\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Visit a Park or Forest\u00a0\u00a0<\/b><\/p>\n

There are so many gorgeous parks and forests throughout greater Boston that you can visit while still practicing social distancing. Whether you want to go on a hike, nature walk, mountain biking, or just sight see, there\u2019s something for you.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

For a more scenic trip that takes you through the city, try the Freedom Trail, which showcases Boston\u2019s historic past and some truly beautiful neighborhoods. The Boston Harbor and Castle Island loop is perfect for a scenic afternoon walk with a great view of the skyline.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

If you\u2019re looking for a bit more activity though check out Blue Hills Reservation just outside of the city. There are dozens of great trails for hiking, running, mountain biking, and more.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

**For a more detailed list of the best trails around the city, check out AllTrails.com.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Have a Socially Distant Picnic\u00a0<\/b><\/p>\n

A great way to eat out and see your friends and family, while still being socially distant, is to have a picnic! Grab your favorite food and drinks as takeout (or pack your own for a more budget friendly option), and a blanket, and call up your friends and family. Seeing them in person, even from 6 ft apart, is amazing. Trust us!<\/span><\/p>\n

Photo: LiseGa2015 via TripAdvisor<\/p><\/div>\n

Camping\u00a0<\/b><\/p>\n

While many traditional vacations have been cancelled, it doesn\u2019t mean you can\u2019t still get away! There are plenty of campsites in Massachusetts and around the country that are open for business. You can spend your time swinging in hammocks, swimming, stargazing, and connecting with nature.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

Alternative option: camp in your backyard. If you want the comfort of staying at home, but still want a new experience, pitch a tent in the backyard for a few nights, make a campfire, and look up at the stars. It can be just as fun!<\/span><\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Take a Road Trip\u00a0<\/b><\/p>\n

Taking a road trip is another classic vacation that can still be done this summer. There is so much of this country to see, so why not start now? Whether you\u2019re interested in seeing the mountains, the beach, the forest, or a new city, there are countless road trips that you can take. The only limit is your imagination. <\/span><\/p>\n
